Intro

These three essays form a continuous spectrum of design critique, moving from the emotional charge of aesthetics, to the instability of identity systems, to the long-standing debates around style itself. Read together, they map out a gradient of perspectives on how designers create, interpret, and negotiate meaning. The site’s shifting color fields and modular layout mirror this conceptual continuum, framing each text as one hue within a larger “Spectrum of Style.”

Colophon

This project was designed and developed by Sooim Kang in 2025. Headings are set in DM Sans, and body text is set in Rethink Sans, chosen for their clarity and contemporary neutrality. The visual system uses a custom gradient palette—#FFCA94 (orange), #FFE182 (yellow), #9BBF6B (green), #A1DDEC (blue), #DCCDFA (purple), #F4C7DA (pink)—each transitioning into white to maintain accessibility against black text. The collection includes three essays: “Hooked on a Feeling” by Michael Rock (2015), “Post-Identity Design” by Federico Pérez Villoro & Christopher Hamamoto (2017), and “Style Is Not a Four-Letter Word” by Jeffery Keedy (2004). The full digital layout, animations, and responsive grid were created specifically to unify these writings within a coherent editorial identity.
